# Harmonia — design spec extension v0.7: Monte-Carlo confidence intervals on the flip frequency

**Status:** **proposed / implemented in v0.7.0** (2026-06-10) · extends [`spec.md`](../../spec.md) (v0.1)
· hardens the **headline output** (§5, §6) — the classification-flip frequency
· **adds no new modeling, sources no data, touches no calibrated threshold, and
changes no previously-reported number; it reports the sampling uncertainty of a
number Harmonia already reports.**

> *Harmonia's thesis is "never report a number without its uncertainty" (spec.md
> §5–6, §10). The classification-flip frequency is the project's headline number —
> and until v0.7 it was reported as a bare point estimate, even though it is a
> **Monte-Carlo binomial proportion** with its own sampling error that shrinks only
> as `n_mc` grows. v0.7 applies the project's own principle reflexively: every
> reported flip frequency (and the population susceptible fraction) now carries a
> **Wilson score 95% confidence interval** derived from its Monte-Carlo sample
> size. The Sobol indices already report bootstrap standard errors; the headline
> flip frequency now matches that standard.*

---

## 0. Why this, and why now

The flip frequency answers "how often does the high/intermediate/low call change
as I resample the published IC50s?" It is estimated by counting flips over `n_mc`
Monte-Carlo draws, so it is itself a statistic: `flip_freq = k / n_mc` for `k`
observed flips. At the default `n_mc = 200`, a reported `36%` carries a 95%
interval of roughly `30–43%` — wide enough that two drugs reported at `30%` and
`40%` may not be distinguishable at that budget. Reporting `36%` alone invites the
reader to over-trust a noisy estimate, which is precisely the failure mode the
whole project exists to prevent.

The fix is small, exact, and entirely in-thesis: attach the Monte-Carlo
confidence interval to the proportion. It requires no new science — only the
honest bookkeeping the project already demands of its inputs.

---

## 1. Scope

**In scope — the math (a standard, well-behaved interval).**

- A pure helper `wilson_interval(k, n, z=Z95)` returning the **Wilson score
  interval** for a binomial proportion `k/n`, and a thin
  `flip_ci(freq, n, z=Z95)` that recovers `k = round(freq·n)` and delegates to it.
  The Wilson interval is chosen over the normal (Wald) approximation because it is
  **bounded to [0, 1]** and **non-degenerate at the extremes** (`k = 0` and
  `k = n`), which is exactly where flip frequencies live (a tight HIGH blocker
  flips `0/200` times; the Wald interval would report a misleading zero-width
  interval there, the Wilson interval reports an honest upper bound).

**In scope — the surfaces (every reported proportion).**

- `RiskAssessment.flip_ci` — the 95% CI of `classification_flip_frequency`.
- `RiskAssessment.reproducibility_flip_ci` — the CI of the new-lab predictive flip
  frequency (`uq="bayes"`; `(nan, nan)` under `uq="moments"`).
- `CombinationAssessment.flip_ci` — the CI of the combination's joint-variability
  flip frequency.
- `FlipSensitivity.all_vary_flip_ci` — the CI of the all-channels-vary flip
  frequency (the per-channel solo/frozen attributions stay point estimates — they
  are a *ranking*, not a reported headline rate).
- `PopulationAssessment.susceptible_fraction_ci` — the CI of the susceptible
  fraction over the `n_models` virtual myocytes (a binomial proportion of the same
  kind).

Each `summary()` appends the interval inline next to its proportion, e.g.

```
classification-flip frequency: 36% (95% CI 30–43%, 200 MC draws)
```

**Out of scope (declared).**

- **Changing any number.** `classification_flip_frequency`,
  `susceptible_fraction`, the class-probability distribution, every qNet / ΔAPD90 /
  cqInward value, and all calibrated thresholds are byte-identical with v0.6.x. The
  CI is an additional field; the `n_mc = 0` "point-estimate only" path reports
  `(nan, nan)` (no sampling, no interval).
- **Per-class CIs.** Only the headline flip frequency (and susceptible fraction)
  get an interval; annotating each of the three class probabilities would clutter
  the summary without changing the decision the CI supports ("is my flip frequency
  resolved enough to compare?").
- **Adaptive `n_mc`.** v0.7 reports the interval; it does not auto-grow the sample
  to hit a target width. The interval makes the budget's adequacy *visible*, which
  is the honest minimum; choosing the budget stays the caller's decision.

## 2. The interval

For `k` flips in `n` Monte-Carlo draws, `p̂ = k/n`, and `z` the standard-normal
quantile (`Z95 = 1.959963984540054` for 95%):

```
center = (p̂ + z²/2n) / (1 + z²/n)
half   = (z / (1 + z²/n)) · sqrt( p̂(1−p̂)/n + z²/4n² )
CI     = [ max(0, center − half),  min(1, center + half) ]
```

`n ≤ 0` ⇒ `(nan, nan)`. The interval is symmetric in coverage, asymmetric on the
probability scale near the extremes (correctly: a `0/200` estimate has CI
`[0, 1.9%]`, not `[0, 0]`).

---

## 3. Guardrails — unchanged

Every v0.1 §10 guardrail holds verbatim. The CI **strengthens** the no-verdict
posture: it makes explicit that the headline number is an estimate with error, not
an exact fact, so it cannot be mistaken for an authoritative rate. It introduces no
new claim, sources no data, and reports nothing about a drug — only about the
precision of a Monte-Carlo computation.

The tell that this layer crossed its line would be quoting the CI as a clinical
confidence in a safety call. It is not that; it is the sampling error of a
resampling experiment, and the summaries say so.

---

## 4. Validation & verification contract (CI gates)

| Guard | What it proves | Mechanism |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Wilson math** | matches hand-computed values for known `(k, n)`; equals the textbook interval; `Z95` is the 95% quantile | `tests/test_flip_ci.py` |
| **Bounds & extremes** | the interval ⊆ `[0, 1]`; at `k=0` and `k=n` it is non-degenerate (Wald would be zero-width); contains `p̂` | unit test |
| **Convergence** | the interval half-width shrinks ∝ `1/√n` as `n_mc` grows (narrower at 2000 than at 50) | unit test |
| **Contains the point** | every reported `flip_ci` brackets its `classification_flip_frequency`; same for susceptible fraction | integration test |
| **Non-drift** | `classification_flip_frequency`, `susceptible_fraction`, the class distribution, and every metric value are byte-identical with v0.6.x; the CI is purely additive; `n_mc=0` ⇒ `(nan, nan)` | backward-compat test |
| **Reproducibility** | dataset + text exports regenerate byte-identically (only the `datasetVersion` stamp moves to 0.7.0) | CI `git diff --exit-code` |

*v0.7 closes the most ironic remaining gap in the project: the headline
flip-frequency, the number the whole dataset exists to compute, was reported
without the sampling uncertainty the project demands of every other number. It now
carries a Wilson 95% confidence interval — exact, bounded, well-behaved at the
extremes where flip frequencies live — across every surface that reports a
Monte-Carlo proportion, while changing no previously-reported value and relaxing no
guardrail. It is the project's own principle, turned on its own output.*
