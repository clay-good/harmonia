# Harmonia — design spec extension v0.2: Bayesian dose-response uncertainty quantification

**Status:** **implemented in v0.2.0** (2026-06-09) · extends [`spec.md`](../../spec.md) (v0.1) ·
roadmap Phase C ("Variability layer") · **does not relax any §10 guardrail.**

> **Implementation note (what shipped vs. what this spec proposed).** The inference
> layer ([`harmonia.infer`](../../python/harmonia/infer.py)), the `uq="bayes"`
> drop-in ([`assess`](../../python/harmonia/simulate.py)), the one-sided censored
> posterior, the prior registry ([`dataset/priors/`](../../dataset/priors)), the
> per-channel `prior_sensitivity` / `identifiability_score`, and the Sobol sensitivity
> (`flip_sensitivity(method="sobol")`) all shipped. Two deliberate, simplifying
> departures, justified in-line below: (1) the hierarchical posterior is computed by an
> **exact direct (grid + conjugate) sampler** in pure NumPy rather than a PPL/MCMC —
> the summary regime is small enough that this is faster, dependency-free, and free of
> the Neal-funnel pathology, and `rhat`/`ess` are still reported; (2) the
> `posterior_summary` is **not cached into the record files** — it is recomputed on
> demand (milliseconds) so the source of truth stays `(source data + prior)`, and
> reproducibility is asserted by a "run twice → byte-identical" test rather than a
> cross-platform `git diff` gate. SBC (§9) is approximated by the recovery/reduction
> and coverage-style checks in [`tests/test_infer.py`](../../tests/test_infer.py); the
> raw dose-response data layer (§3 raw regime, C-UQ-5) is schema-supported and
> forward-compatible but not yet populated, since no record has been human-verified to
> the raw-points standard the spec requires.

> *Where v0.1 made input variability a first-class **field**, v0.2 makes it a
> first-class **inference**: the spread of an IC50 stops being a number transcribed
> from a table and becomes a posterior derived — under a declared prior — from the
> underlying dose-response data, with the unidentifiable case (max block < ~60%)
> handled as a **one-sided censored posterior** rather than a binary exclusion gate.*

This document specifies the statistical machinery, the schema deltas, the
validation contract, and the guardrails for that change. It is written to the
same standard as the v0.1 spec: every claim either reduces provably to the
existing behavior, or is gated behind an explicit, tier-honest declaration.

---

## 0. Why this, and why now

Harmonia's load-bearing thesis (v1.0 §16) is that *a model is only as trustworthy
as its weakest, least-validated input.* The v0.1 release operationalized that with
`source_values` + `variability` and a Monte-Carlo that propagates the inter-source
spread to a **classification-flip frequency**. That machinery is correct and is the
durable contribution. But the *sampling distribution it propagates* is, today, a
method-of-moments approximation, and it discards information in three places that
matter scientifically. v0.2 closes all three.

### 0.1 The three gaps in the current sampler

The present Monte-Carlo ([`simulate.py:_channel_draws`](../../python/harmonia/simulate.py))
builds, per identifiable channel, a lognormal IC50 draw:

```python
mu    = log10(geomean(source_ic50s))           # center
sigma = max(std(log10(source_ic50s), ddof=1), 0.03)   # spread, ≥2 sources
        or DEFAULT_SINGLE_SOURCE_SIGMA                 # spread, 1 source
hill  = b.hill                                          # fixed point value
```

| # | Gap | Consequence today | v0.2 fix |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1** | **Spread is a point estimate from 2–3 numbers.** `std(ddof=1)` of three log-IC50s is itself a wildly uncertain quantity, and `DEFAULT_SINGLE_SOURCE_SIGMA` is a hard-coded prior masquerading as data. | The flip frequency has its own (unreported) sampling error; single-source channels carry a magic constant. | A **hierarchical posterior** that (a) borrows the dataset's *learned* between-lab variance for sparse channels and (b) carries the uncertainty-in-the-uncertainty honestly. |
| **2** | **The Hill coefficient is fixed.** Block steepness is propagated as a point value even when sources disagree on it. | Sub-saturating exposures (the validation-set regime, README §"performance") are exactly where Hill uncertainty changes the block fraction — and it is silently set to zero. | Joint `(IC50, h)` posterior; Hill uncertainty propagates into the block factor. |
| **3** | **Sub-60%-block is a binary gate.** A channel with `max_block_observed < 60%` is *excluded* and the assessment caps at Tier D. | A measurement of "45% block at the highest tested dose" carries real information — *IC50 is above roughly the top dose* — and it is thrown away entirely. The stored extrapolated point IC50 (e.g. `ranolazine.ical` = 296 000 nM) is provenance-only and unusable. | A **censored (one-sided) posterior**: the max-block observation becomes a likelihood that bounds IC50 from below, yielding a heavy right tail instead of nothing. The hard tier gate is *preserved* as a guardrail (§6), but the channel's residual block is no longer silently set to "none." |

None of these is a bug — v0.1 made the right call to ship the simple sampler first.
v0.2 is the principled version the uncertainty-quantification literature
([`chang-2017`](../../dataset/citations/chang-2017.json); Mirams-group hierarchical
Bayesian IC50 work, §12) already established and that — exactly as the README says
of the v0.1 finding — *no dataset has operationalized.*

### 0.2 The non-drift guarantee (the family signature)

The new machinery **must reduce to the old one in the limit it was valid for.** In
the well-identified, many-source, agreed-Hill regime the posterior mean of
`log10(IC50)` converges to the log-geomean and the posterior predictive SD
converges to the sample SD. This is a *testable* property (§9, `recovery`), and it
is the v0.2 analog of every other "provable non-drift" guard in the project
(README §"Validation & testing"). A reviewer can trust v0.2 precisely because it
does not silently move the v0.1 numbers where v0.1 was already right.

## 1. Scope of this extension

**In scope**

- A **dose-response data layer**: optional raw `(concentration, fractional_block)`
  points (or per-source summary statistics) attached to a `source_value`, sufficient
  to *infer* an IC50/Hill posterior rather than transcribe a point.
- A **hierarchical Bayesian inference** of the per-channel `(IC50, Hill)` posterior
  from those data, with partial pooling of the between-lab variance across the dataset.
- A **censored likelihood** for sub-saturating block, producing a one-sided
  posterior on IC50 instead of an exclusion.
- **Posterior-predictive sampling** that drops into the existing Monte-Carlo in
  place of the method-of-moments lognormal draw.
- **Global, variance-based sensitivity** (first-order and total-effect Sobol
  indices) over the joint posterior, generalizing the current one-at-a-time
  `flip_sensitivity` (README §"Which input drives the flip?").
- The **prior registry**: every prior is a declared, citable, version-pinned object;
  prior influence is itself reportable (§7).

**Out of scope (unchanged from v0.1 §2, restated because they bound this work)**

- Any safety **verdict**. v0.2 produces a *richer* distribution and flip frequency;
  it never collapses them to a determination. The §10 hard line is untouched.
- De novo channel biophysics. We infer pharmacology parameters; we do not re-derive
  the Hill/Markov model structure.
- A full Markov state-dependent binding posterior (that belongs to the separate
  "full CiPA Markov hERG" thread; this spec infers the *Hill / static* parameters
  and the v0.1 Langmuir `dynamic_binding` IC50, and is forward-compatible with it).
- Replacing the reliability gate. The 60%-block tier cap stays (§6); v0.2 only stops
  *discarding the information* the gate currently throws away.

## 2. The statistical model

### 2.1 The measurement model (per source)

A patch-clamp concentration–response experiment for drug *d* on channel *c* at
source *s* measures fractional block *y* at concentrations *x*. The Hill model is

```
f(x; IC50, h) = x^h / (x^h + IC50^h)          # fraction of current blocked, in [0,1]
```

Two data regimes, both supported (a source provides whichever it has):

- **Raw regime** — the source reports points `{(x_i, y_i, n_i, sem_i)}`. Likelihood:
  `y_i ~ Normal(f(x_i; IC50_s, h_s), σ_i)` truncated to `[0,1]`, with `σ_i` from the
  reported SEM (or a source-level σ when SEM is absent). A Beta likelihood is an
  allowed alternative for proportion data (declared per source).
- **Summary regime** — the source reports only a fitted `(IC50_s, hill_s)` and a
  `max_block_observed_percent`. The fitted IC50 is treated as a noisy observation of
  the source's true log-IC50: `log10(IC50_s^obs) ~ Normal(log10(IC50_s), σ_fit)`,
  with `σ_fit` defaulting to the dataset-learned within-source fit SD. **This is the
  regime every v0.1 record is in today** — so v0.2 runs on the existing dataset with
  no new data, and the raw regime is the upgrade path for verified records.

### 2.2 The hierarchical (partial-pooling) structure

The scientific quantity of interest is the drug–channel's *true* pharmacological
IC50, of which each lab sees a noisy, possibly biased realization. Model the
per-source true log-IC50 as drawn from a channel-level distribution:

```
log10(IC50_s)  ~  Normal( μ_c , τ_c )          # s = 1..n_sources  (between-lab)
μ_c            ~  Normal( m_0 , s_0 )          # channel-level true log-IC50
τ_c            ~  HalfNormal( τ_pop )          # between-lab SD, partially pooled
τ_pop          ~  HalfNormal( σ_τ )            # dataset-wide hyperprior on lab spread
h_s            ~  LogNormal( log(μ_h,c) , τ_h )
```

The hierarchy buys the two things the method-of-moments sampler cannot:

1. **Sparse-channel borrowing.** A single-source channel does not get a hard-coded
   `DEFAULT_SINGLE_SOURCE_SIGMA`. Its IC50 uncertainty is the posterior under the
   **dataset-learned** typical between-lab spread `τ_pop` — i.e. "channels like this
   one usually vary lab-to-lab by *this* much." A magic constant becomes an inferred,
   citable quantity, and the more multi-source channels the dataset accrues, the
   better-calibrated every single-source channel becomes. This is the load-bearing
   thesis turned reflexive: the dataset quantifies its own variability prior.
2. **Uncertainty in the spread.** `τ_c` is sampled, not fixed, so the flip frequency
   inherits the (real, currently hidden) uncertainty in how much sources disagree.

### 2.3 The censored likelihood (the unidentifiability fix)

When source *s* observed a maximum block `m_max` (a fraction) at its highest tested
concentration `x_max`, and `m_max` is below the saturating regime, the data do **not**
identify an IC50 — but they do impose a bound. Inverting the Hill equation at the top
dose,

```
IC50  =  x_max · ( (1 − m_max) / m_max )^(1/h)
```

so `m_max < 0.5` ⇒ `IC50 > x_max`, and the smaller `m_max`, the further above `x_max`
the IC50 must lie. v0.2 encodes the observation as a **censored likelihood** at the
top dose (`f(x_max) = m_max ± σ`), which under the §2.2 prior yields a posterior with
a hard-ish lower edge near `x_max` and a heavy, prior-shaped right tail. Three honest
consequences, all reported:

- The posterior is **proper but wide** — its influence on the AP simulation is a
  weak, bounded residual block, *not* the zero block the v0.1 exclusion implies and
  *not* the fictional point block the extrapolated 296 000 nM implies.
- Its **prior-sensitivity is high by construction** (the right tail is prior-shaped),
  so v0.2 flags it (§7): a censored channel's contribution is *prior-dominated*, and
  the honest recommendation is the same as v0.1's — go measure it at higher doses.
- It is **still tier-capped at D** (§6). The gate is a guardrail on the *claim*, not
  a license to discard the *information*.

### 2.4 Two distinct posteriors — and which one drives the flip

The hierarchy admits two predictive questions, and conflating them is a classic UQ
error this spec calls out explicitly:

| Question | Posterior to sample | Drives |
| --- | --- | --- |
| *What is the drug's true IC50 on this channel?* | `μ_c` (the channel-level true value) | the **risk distribution** and **flip frequency** — the headline outputs |
| *What would the next lab measure?* | posterior **predictive** of a new `IC50_s` (adds `τ_c`) | a **reproducibility** readout: "how much would a fresh replication move the call?" |

v0.2's headline flip frequency samples `μ_c` (true-value uncertainty). The new-lab
predictive is offered as a *separate, labeled* readout (`reproducibility_flip`), never
mixed into the primary number. Both are distributions; neither is a verdict.

## 3. The dose-response data layer (schema delta)

A backward-compatible, **optional** extension to the `source_values` item and a new
optional `inference` block on the channel-block record. Records without the new
fields fall back to the §2.1 summary regime, so **the existing 68 channel-block
records validate and simulate unchanged.**

```jsonc
"source_values": [
  {
    "ic50_nm": 4.9, "hill": 0.9, "platform": "manual", "citation": "crumb-2016",
    "temperature_c": 37, "max_block_observed_percent": 95,

    // NEW (all optional) — the dose-response data layer
    "dose_response": {                     // raw regime: enables true inference
      "concentration_nm": [1, 3, 10, 30, 100],
      "fractional_block":  [0.10, 0.27, 0.55, 0.78, 0.91],
      "n_cells":           [6, 6, 5, 6, 6],
      "sem":               [0.03, 0.04, 0.05, 0.04, 0.03],
      "likelihood": "truncated_normal",    // truncated_normal | beta
      "top_dose_censored": false           // true ⇒ §2.3 one-sided likelihood
    },
    "fit_sd_log10": 0.08                   // summary regime: per-source σ_fit if known
  }
],

"inference": {                             // NEW — the per-channel posterior contract
  "method": "hierarchical_bayes",          // hierarchical_bayes | method_of_moments (v0.1 fallback)
  "prior": "harmonia-ic50-prior-v1",       // FK into the prior registry (§7)
  "censored": false,                        // any source top-dose-censored ⇒ true
  "posterior_summary": {                    // CACHED, regenerated by build step — never hand-edited
    "log10_ic50_nm": { "mean": 0.704, "sd": 0.071, "q05": 0.59, "q95": 0.82 },
    "hill":          { "mean": 0.90,  "sd": 0.06 },
    "rhat_max": 1.003, "ess_min": 1850,    // sampler diagnostics, asserted in CI (§9)
    "prior_sensitivity": 0.04              // §7: fraction of posterior variance attributable to the prior
  }
}
```

Design rules, in the family tradition (README §"The record"):

- **The posterior summary is a generated projection, not a curated input.** It is
  produced by a deterministic `build_posteriors.py` step (the analog of
  `build_records.py`) from the raw/summary data + the named prior, and CI checks it
  regenerates byte-identically (seeded sampler). It is cached in the record only so
  that `harmonia.load()` stays a zero-dependency read; the *source of truth* is
  `(dose_response | summary) + prior`.
- **`method: "method_of_moments"`** is the explicit v0.1 fallback. A record with no
  `dose_response` and no `fit_sd_log10` is inferred under the summary regime; a record
  that opts out entirely keeps the exact v0.1 lognormal draw. Selectable globally
  (`assess(..., uq="bayes" | "moments")`) so every v0.2 number is diffable against
  its v0.1 counterpart.

## 4. Posterior-predictive sampling — the drop-in

The Monte-Carlo loop is unchanged in shape; only the per-channel draw changes. Today:

```python
ic50_draw = 10 ** rng.normal(mu_log10, sigma_log10)     # method of moments
hill      = b.hill                                       # fixed
```

v0.2 (`uq="bayes"`):

```python
# draw a joint posterior sample of (true log10 IC50, hill) for this channel
k          = rng.integers(n_posterior_draws)             # index a cached posterior sample set
ic50_draw  = 10 ** posterior.log10_ic50[k]               # true-value posterior (§2.4)
hill       = posterior.hill[k]                            # Hill uncertainty now propagates
```

Because the posterior draws are **shared, indexed sample sets** with a fixed seed,
common-random-numbers across channels is preserved exactly as the current
`flip_sensitivity` requires (README §"sensitivity attribution"), so solo/frozen
scenarios stay comparable and the whole computation stays deterministic and
CI-reproducible. Censored channels (§2.3) contribute their wide one-sided draws
rather than being skipped, and the assessment still reports them in
`excluded_channels`→ renamed `censored_channels` with the tier cap intact.

## 5. Global sensitivity — from one-at-a-time to variance-based

The v0.1 `flip_sensitivity` re-runs the Monte-Carlo with one channel varying
("solo") and one frozen ("frozen"). That is a one-at-a-time (OAT) design: it reads
*main effects* well but **cannot see interactions** between channels (e.g. an IKr–ICaL
trade-off where neither IC50 alone flips the call but their joint variation does).
v0.2 formalizes the attribution as **variance-based (Sobol) sensitivity** over the
joint posterior:

| Index | Meaning | v0.1 analog |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `S_i` (first-order) | fraction of the flip-outcome variance explained by channel *i*'s IC50 alone | ≈ `solo_flip_frequency` |
| `S_Ti` (total-effect) | fraction explained by *i* including all its interactions | ≈ `frozen_flip_frequency` (complement) |
| `S_Ti − S_i` | the **interaction load** on channel *i* — invisible to OAT | *(new)* |

Implementation note (kept honest, no over-claim): the flip outcome is binary, so the
relevant decomposition is of the variance of the indicator (or, better-behaved, of the
continuous qNet/ΔAPD90 metric, with the flip reported alongside). Estimator is the
standard Saltelli/Sobol design or, for the binary case, a Hoeffding/ANOVA
decomposition; the spec does not mandate one, only that first-order and total-effect
indices are reported with their Monte-Carlo standard errors. The v0.1 OAT readout
remains available and is documented as the cheap approximation that Sobol generalizes.
The dominant-driver recommendation ("pin this IC50 down first") is unchanged in spirit
but now **interaction-aware**: a channel can be the dominant *total-effect* driver
while having a small *solo* effect, and that is exactly the case OAT misses.

## 6. Tier semantics — continuous identifiability, hard gate preserved

v0.1 identifiability is binary: `max_block ≥ 60%` ⟺ identifiable ⟺ not-Tier-D. v0.2
adds a **continuous identifiability readout** *without removing the hard gate*:

- **Continuous readout** — `identifiability_score`, e.g. the posterior coefficient of
  variation of IC50, or `P(IC50 < 10× top tested dose)`. This makes "how unidentifiable"
  a reported number, so a 58%-block channel (just under the gate) and a 20%-block
  channel are no longer indistinguishable.
- **Hard gate, preserved** — `max_block < 60%` **still** forces Tier D and a
  `known_failure_mode`, and still caps any assessment that touches the channel at D.
  The reliability gate is a guardrail on the *claim* (§10), and a continuous score must
  not be allowed to launder a sub-threshold channel into a higher tier. The two coexist:
  the gate governs the **tier**; the score governs the **reported diagnostic**.
- **Prior-dominance flag** — when `prior_sensitivity` (§7) exceeds a declared threshold,
  the channel's posterior is *prior-shaped, not data-shaped*; the record is flagged and
  the assessment warns. A censored channel (§2.3) is prior-dominated by construction.

The worst-input-wins propagation rule (v0.1 §5) is untouched.

## 7. The prior registry — priors are declared inputs, not hidden choices

A Bayesian layer is only as honest as its priors. v0.2 makes every prior a
**first-class, version-pinned, citable object** under `dataset/priors/`, referenced by
key from each record's `inference.prior`. This is the tier philosophy applied to the
inference itself: a prior is an input, and an undeclared input is exactly what this
project exists to refuse.

```jsonc
// dataset/priors/harmonia-ic50-prior-v1.json
{
  "id": "harmonia-ic50-prior-v1",
  "channel_level":   { "m_0_log10nm": 3.0, "s_0_log10nm": 2.0 },   // weakly informative on true IC50
  "between_lab_tau": { "dist": "half_normal", "scale_log10": 0.5 }, // lab-spread hyperprior
  "hill":            { "dist": "lognormal", "mu": 0.0, "sigma": 0.3 },
  "rationale": "Weakly-informative; s_0 spans the nM–µM range cardiac blockers occupy. "
               "between-lab scale 0.5 log10 ≈ 3-fold, the documented inter-lab IC50 spread.",
  "citations": ["chang-2017", "kramer-2013"],
  "predictive": false
}
```

Two requirements, both CI-enforced:

1. **Prior-sensitivity is computed and reported.** Each posterior carries
   `prior_sensitivity` — the fraction of posterior variance attributable to the prior
   rather than the data (estimated by re-inference under a perturbed/widened prior, or
   by the prior-vs-posterior variance ratio). A high value is not an error; it is the
   honest statement "this number is mostly prior," and it drives the §6 flag.
2. **The default prior is weakly informative and justified.** No prior may smuggle in a
   risk conclusion. A prior whose mass materially shifts the *classification* (not just
   the metric) must be declared and is itself a Tier-affecting choice.

## 8. API surface (additive, backward-compatible)

```python
import harmonia
ds = harmonia.load()

# v0.1 call is unchanged and still defaults to the moments sampler until v0.2 ships:
res = harmonia.assess(ds, "dofetilide")                    # uq="moments" (v0.1 behavior)

# v0.2: Bayesian posterior-predictive propagation
res = harmonia.assess(ds, "dofetilide", uq="bayes")
res.qnet_distribution                  # now reflects (IC50, Hill) posterior + censoring
res.classification_flip_frequency      # samples the TRUE-value posterior (§2.4)
res.reproducibility_flip_frequency     # NEW: new-lab predictive (§2.4) — labeled, separate
res.censored_channels                  # NEW: channels contributing one-sided posteriors
res.prior_dominated_channels           # NEW: §6/§7 prior-dominance flags

# Per-channel posterior (the inferred object, not a transcribed point)
post = harmonia.posterior(ds, "dofetilide", "IKr")
post.log10_ic50.mean, post.log10_ic50.sd
post.identifiability_score             # §6 continuous readout
post.prior_sensitivity                 # §7

# Global, interaction-aware sensitivity (generalizes flip_sensitivity)
sens = harmonia.flip_sensitivity(ds, "verapamil", method="sobol")
sens.channels[0].first_order           # S_i   (≈ v0.1 solo)
sens.channels[0].total_effect          # S_Ti  (≈ v0.1 frozen-complement)
sens.channels[0].interaction_load      # S_Ti − S_i  (NEW — invisible to OAT)
sens.dominant_channel                  # now interaction-aware
```

```bash
harmonia infer verapamil               # show the per-channel posteriors + diagnostics
harmonia assess verapamil --uq bayes   # Bayesian flip frequency + reproducibility flip
harmonia sensitivity verapamil --sobol # variance-based attribution with MC standard errors
harmonia priors                        # list the prior registry + per-record prior usage
```

## 9. Validation & verification contract (CI gates)

v0.2 inherits every v0.1 guard and adds the inference-specific ones. As always, the
point is *provable non-drift*, not fixtures.

| Guard | What it proves | Mechanism |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Posterior reproducibility** | `build_posteriors.py` regenerates every cached `posterior_summary` byte-identically (seeded sampler) — the posterior *is* a deterministic projection of (data + prior). | CI `git diff --exit-code`, mirrors the v0.1 `build_records.py` guard |
| **Sampler convergence** | every posterior meets `rhat_max < 1.01` and `ess_min` above a floor; CI fails on a non-converged record. | assert over `inference.posterior_summary` |
| **Reduction / non-drift** (§0.2) | in the many-source, agreed-Hill, fully-identified limit the Bayesian posterior mean → log-geomean and predictive SD → sample SD, so `uq="bayes"` and `uq="moments"` agree to tolerance. | synthetic + real well-identified channels (e.g. `dofetilide.ikr`) |
| **Censoring correctness** (§2.3) | a synthetic sub-60%-block dataset yields a proper, one-sided posterior with a lower edge near the top dose and the documented heavy tail; and the channel is **still tier-capped at D**. | synthetic fixture + `ranolazine.ical` |
| **Simulation-based calibration (SBC)** | data simulated from the prior, then re-inferred, produces rank-uniform posteriors — the inference is correctly *implemented*, not merely plausible. | seeded SBC harness, asserted rank histogram |
| **Posterior coverage** | on synthetic identifiable cases, the 90% credible interval covers the true IC50 ≈ 90% of the time. | synthetic recovery sweep |
| **Sobol consistency** (§5) | total-effect ≥ first-order for every channel; indices sum within MC error; the binary-flip decomposition agrees with the metric decomposition in the no-interaction limit. | property test |
| **Prior-sensitivity honesty** (§7) | the reported `prior_sensitivity` matches a recomputation under a perturbed prior within tolerance; a censored channel reads prior-dominated. | re-inference check |
| **Backward compatibility** | every existing record validates against the extended schema; `uq="moments"` reproduces the v0.1 flip frequencies exactly. | full v0.1 suite, unchanged |

**Human verification (the gate on `verified`), extended.** The v0.1 reviewer confirms
the IC50, assay context, and max-block. The v0.2 reviewer additionally confirms, for any
record carrying a `dose_response` block, that the **raw concentration–block points and the
top-dose-censoring flag** match the source figure/table — because that raw layer is now
where the posterior, and therefore the flip frequency, actually comes from. LLMs assist
extraction of the points; they never promote to `verified` and never author a posterior.

## 10. Guardrails — unchanged, and one addition

Every v0.1 §10 guardrail holds verbatim. v0.2 is a *deepening* of the uncertainty
report, never a step toward a verdict; if anything it makes the uncertainty larger and
more honest (Hill spread and censored tails widen the distribution). Two clarifications
specific to this layer:

- **A posterior is not a permission to point-estimate.** v0.2 must never expose a
  posterior *mean* IC50 as "the" value in any context that reads like a determination.
  The outputs remain distributions, flip frequencies, and explicitly-labeled diagnostics.
- **The prior must never carry the conclusion (§7).** The single tell that this layer has
  crossed the line: a prior tuned — by whatever route — so that the *classification*
  follows from the prior rather than the data. Such a prior is rejected in review, and the
  `prior_sensitivity` gate exists precisely to make that visible.

The original tell stands: *any feature that emits a single, authoritative
"this drug is safe/unsafe" verdict without its uncertainty does not get built.*
