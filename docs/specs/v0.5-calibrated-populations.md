# Harmonia — design spec extension v0.5: experimentally-calibrated populations

**Status:** **implemented in v0.5.0** (2026-06-09) · extends [`spec.md`](../../spec.md) (v0.1)
· completes the **experimentally-calibrated populations** half of roadmap Phase E
· **ships hypothesis-tier, NOT FOR PREDICTION, and relaxes no §10 guardrail.**

> *v0.1 gave the `populations` subsystem an inter-individual **variability** cloud
> (`illustrative_v0`); v0.3 added the disease/genetic **mean shift** (LQT1/2/3).
> Both sample the *prior* conductance cloud and accept every draw — including the
> implausible tail where an extreme conductance combination yields a drug-free
> action potential no real myocyte would show. v0.5 adds the landmark
> **experimentally-calibrated populations-of-models** method
> ([Britton et al. 2013](../../dataset/citations/britton-2013.json)): admit a
> virtual myocyte only when its **drug-free** AP biomarkers are physiologically
> plausible. It is the named Phase-E remaining item, and it remains exactly as
> non-predictive as the populations layer it refines.*

---

## 0. Why this, and why now

A population of models is only meaningful if its members could exist. Britton et
al. (2013) established the discipline that makes the method credible: you do not
keep every conductance draw — you **calibrate** the population by rejecting any
candidate whose *drug-free* electrophysiology falls outside experimentally
observed ranges. The surviving population is, by construction, one whose baseline
behaviour is physiological, so the *drug* response it then produces is a spread
over plausible hearts rather than over arithmetic artefacts of the sampler.

Harmonia's v0.1/v0.3 population layer shipped the *sampling* machinery but accepted
every myocyte. Empirically (the reduced kernel, illustrative CVs) that admits a
real abnormal tail: ~8% of drug-free myocytes have pathologically long or
triangular repolarization — triangulation reaching 250–300 ms against a ~42 ms
baseline — i.e. cells that are already abnormal before any drug. v0.5 closes the
Phase-E gap with the minimal honest addition: an **acceptance filter on the
drug-free beat**.

---

## 1. Scope

**In scope**

- A backward-compatible, optional **`calibration`** block on a `population` record:
  accepted ranges for drug-free AP biomarkers (`apd90_ms`, `vrest_mv`, `vpeak_mv`,
  `triangulation_ms`) in the kernel's own units, plus a `max_oversample` termination
  guard and the citing of the method.
- One new population record, **`calibrated_v0`** — the `illustrative_v0` variability
  cloud, admitted through the drug-free acceptance filter.
- The **`calibrate_population`** primitive (returns the accepted myocytes, their
  cached drug-free beats, and the acceptance bookkeeping) and its drop-in into
  `assess_population`: a population record carrying a `calibration` block triggers
  calibration-by-rejection; one without it is the byte-identical prior-cloud path.
  No new CLI surface — `harmonia population <drug> --population calibrated_v0`.

**Out of scope (unchanged from v0.1 §2, restated because they bound this work)**

- Any **predictive** claim. The acceptance ranges are **kernel-plausibility bounds**
  — bounds on *this reduced kernel's* drug-free biomarkers, bracketing its
  physiological bulk — **not a fit to human population electrophysiology data**. The
  *methodology* is Britton 2013; the *numbers* are not. Every calibrated assessment
  is therefore still **Tier D** and stamped **NOT FOR PREDICTION**, exactly like the
  uncalibrated populations.
- Re-deriving the kernel. Calibration selects among myocytes the existing kernel
  produces; it changes no equation, threshold, or conductance baseline.
- Disease-background calibration. Combining the v0.3 disease mean shift with a
  drug-free acceptance filter (calibrating a *diseased* baseline against *its own*
  plausible range) is a coherent later thread; v0.5 ships the healthy calibrated
  population only.

## 2. The model — acceptance on the drug-free beat

For each candidate myocyte *m*, draw the per-channel conductance multiplier exactly
as before (the v0.3 form `g_{m,c} = s_c · λ_{m,c} · g_c^{healthy}`, here with the
disease shift `s_c = 1`), simulate the **drug-free** action potential, and **accept**
*m* iff it repolarizes (no EAD, no repolarization failure) **and** every calibrated
biomarker lies in its range:

```
accept(m)  ⟺  repolarizes(m)  ∧  ∀ k ∈ calibration:  min_k ≤ biomarker_k(m) ≤ max_k
```

Candidates are drawn until `n` are accepted or the `n · max_oversample` cap is hit
(the termination/determinism guard). The accepted myocyte's drug-free beat is
**cached** and reused as the APD90-metric baseline, so calibration adds no extra
simulation to that path. The drug is then assessed against exactly the accepted
population, unchanged.

The acceptance ranges that ship with `calibrated_v0` (kernel-plausibility bounds;
baseline APD90 ≈ 272 ms, triangulation ≈ 42 ms):

| Biomarker | Accept range | Rejects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `apd90_ms` | [180, 360] | too-short / pathologically-long plateau |
| `vrest_mv` | [−90, −80] | depolarized / hyperpolarized rest (sanity bound) |
| `vpeak_mv` | [40, 70] | failed/over-driven upstroke (sanity bound) |
| `triangulation_ms` | [10, 120] | **the abnormal long/triangular repolarization tail** |

Empirically these admit ≈92% of drug-free myocytes; the **triangulation** bound is
the dominant filter, which is mechanistically right — high drug-free triangulation
is itself a repolarization-instability marker, exactly the abnormality calibration
should remove. The resting/peak bounds rarely bind (the kernel's rest and upstroke
are tight); they are sanity guards, not the working filter.

## 3. Schema delta (backward-compatible)

One new optional property on the `population` object; every existing population
record validates unchanged.

```jsonc
"population": {
  "name": "Experimentally-calibrated population (drug-free AP biomarker acceptance, v0)",
  "n_default": 100,
  "conductance_cv": { "IKr": 0.22, "IKs": 0.28, ... },
  "calibration": {                              // NEW (optional)
    "method": "drug-free AP biomarker acceptance / calibration-by-rejection (Britton 2013)",
    "citation": "britton-2013",
    "max_oversample": 40,                       // candidates drawn per accepted myocyte, capped
    "biomarkers": {
      "apd90_ms":         { "min": 180, "max": 360 },
      "vrest_mv":         { "min": -90, "max": -80 },
      "vpeak_mv":         { "min": 40,  "max": 70 },
      "triangulation_ms": { "min": 10,  "max": 120 }
    }
  },
  "predictive": false                            // still const false — never predictive
}
```

A population with no `calibration` (`illustrative_v0`, `lqt1/2/3`) is identical to
its prior behavior.

## 4. Guardrails — unchanged

Every v0.1 §10 guardrail holds verbatim. Calibration is a *tightening of the
population to physiological plausibility*, never a step toward a verdict or a
prediction:

- **Tier D, always.** A calibrated-population assessment is capped at D and carries
  NOT FOR PREDICTION. The schema pins `predictive: false`.
- **Kernel-plausibility, not patient-fit.** The record's notes and every assessment
  warning state plainly that the acceptance ranges are bounds on the reduced
  kernel's own biomarkers, not a calibration to human population electrophysiology;
  the calibration buys physiological plausibility, not predictiveness.
- **Thresholds untouched.** The drug-response qNet/ΔAPD90 thresholds remain the
  healthy reference; calibration changes which myocytes are in the population, not
  how any of them is classified.
- **No verdict.** The output remains a *distribution* of classifications and a
  susceptible fraction, plus the acceptance bookkeeping — never a determination.

The tell that this layer has crossed its line: any output that reads as a patient-
or population-specific safety prediction. That does not get built.

## 5. Validation & verification contract (CI gates)

| Guard | What it proves | Mechanism |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Acceptance correctness** | every accepted myocyte's drug-free biomarkers fall in the declared ranges and it repolarizes | re-check each accepted beat (unit test) |
| **Rejection happens** | over a full population the raw prior's abnormal tail forces over-sampling, `acceptance_rate < 1`, and the rejected count is non-zero | property test |
| **Triangulation dominates** | the dominant filter is the long/triangular-repolarization bound, not the sanity bounds | rejection-reason ordering test |
| **Tail removed** | the raw prior cloud demonstrably contains out-of-range/abnormal myocytes; the calibrated population contains none | comparison test |
| **APD90 path** | the cached drug-free baseline gives a finite ΔAPD90 distribution under calibration | assess-level test |
| **Tier / non-prediction** | every calibrated assessment is Tier D, the summary carries CALIBRATED + NOT FOR PREDICTION + acceptance bookkeeping | assertion |
| **Determinism** | same seed ⇒ identical accepted set, acceptance rate, and qNet distribution | seeded test |
| **Backward compatibility** | the uncalibrated path is byte-identical (same RNG sequence via the extracted `_draw_multiplier`); every record validates against the extended schema | full v0.1/v0.3 suite + RNG-replication test |
| **Dataset reproducibility** | `calibrated_v0` regenerates byte-identically from `build_records.py` | CI `git diff --exit-code` |

**Human verification (the gate on `verified`).** As everywhere in Harmonia, the
acceptance ranges are LLM/maintainer-authored kernel-plausibility bounds and the
record ships `unverified`; promoting it means a contributor confirms the ranges are
the intended plausibility envelope. LLMs assist but never promote.
