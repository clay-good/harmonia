**Status:** **implemented in v0.8.4** (2026-06-23) · extends
[v0.8](v0.8-machine-crosscheck.md) · resolves the two cross-check follow-ons v0.8
declared as future work · **adds no modeling and changes no risk number.**

> *v0.8 §1 deferred two cross-checks as "natural follow-ons": the `cipa_binding`
> dynamic-hERG kinetics (against the FDA/CiPA `hERG_fitting` optimal fits) and
> `max_block_observed_percent` (against the raw Crumb 2016 dose-response). v0.8.4

## 1. cipa_binding cross-check (implemented)

The v0.6 `cipa_binding` field stores the FDA/CiPA optimized IKr-Markov drug-binding
fit (`Kmax`, `Ku`, `n`, `halfmax`, `Vhalf`) for the 12 CiPA dynamic-fit drugs,
transcribed from `hERG_fitting/results/<drug>/pars.txt`. v0.8.4 adds:

- **`dataset/references/cipa_binding_reference.json`** — those 12 fits, sourced
  from the FDA/CiPA repo (commit `e881df6`) and **verified value-by-value against
  the raw `pars.txt` files when authored** (all 12 × 5 = 60 fields exact).
- **`harmonia.cross_check_binding(ds)` → `BindingReport`** — confirms each stored
  `cipa_binding` field matches the reference within a tight relative tolerance
  (1e-3). Surfaced as a second section of `harmonia crosscheck`.

Unlike the IC50 cross-check (cross-source, fold-tolerant — different labs
legitimately disagree several-fold), this is a **same-source transcription check**:
the values were taken *from* these fits, so any mismatch is a typo, not a
scientific difference. Result: **12/12 drugs transcribe exactly** — the v0.6
sourcing has no transcription error. This is reported, not assumed, and guarded by
a test (`tests/test_crosscheck.py::test_cipa_binding_transcribes_source_exactly`).

It remains a machine cross-check, **not** a `verified` stamp (§9).

## 2. max_block cross-check (investigated; resolved as deferred-to-human)

The intent was to diff each record's `max_block_observed_percent` against the
maximum block actually observed in the raw Crumb 2016 dose-response
(`mergedpatchclampdata`). On inspection this is **not a sound automated check**:

- The raw panel tests a **fixed, often sub-saturating concentration range**. For
  many drug-channel pairs the highest tested dose does not reach the IC50, so the
  raw "max observed block" *systematically understates* the true maximum. Example:
  dofetilide hERG is a near-complete blocker (stored 95%), but the Crumb panel only
  dosed it to ~70% block — so a naive check would flag a "discrepancy" that is just
  the panel's concentration ceiling, not a data error.
- Across 41 comparable records, 20 differ >15 pp from the raw maximum — almost all
  in the `stored > raw` direction, i.e. the expected understatement, not errors.
- Records also draw `max_block` from the specific cited source assay (Kramer, Li,
  or a higher-dose Crumb run), which need not be the merged panel.

Forcing a fold-flag here would manufacture false positives and undermine the
zero-divergence guarantee the IC50 cross-check earns. So `max_block` is **left to
human verification** (the PDF read that promotes a record to `verified`), with this
rationale recorded rather than a misleading automated gate. (Where `max_block` *was*
demonstrably wrong — cisapride/terfenadine — it surfaced through the IC50 divergence
and the unidentifiability gate, and was fixed in v0.8.1 against the primary source.)

## 3. Non-drift

One reference file, one module function, one CLI section, one public export, one
test. No risk number, threshold, classification, record value, or export value
changes. The IC50 cross-check and its zero-divergence guarantee are untouched.
